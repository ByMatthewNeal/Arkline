# ArkLine Twitter System

Practical, not exhaustive. Designed for one founder who has to ship + sell + post + sleep, not a content team.

## Two accounts, two roles

You'll get more traction running this on two parallel tracks rather than just the brand handle.

- **@MatthewNeal (or whatever your personal handle is)** — the human. Build-in-public, hot takes, voice. This is where audience grows. People follow people, not products.
- **@Arklineio** — the brand. Quieter. Retweets your best stuff. Posts product announcements. Replies to support inquiries. Acts as the "official" anchor.

**Posting ratio:** 80% from personal, 20% from brand. The brand account amplifies the personal account, not the other way around.

## The 5 content pillars

Rotate through these. You don't need to hit all five every week, but over a month you should hit each at least 2-3 times.

| Pillar | What it sounds like | Cadence target |
|---|---|---|
| **Build updates** | "Shipped X today. Here's what was harder than expected." | 2-3/week |
| **Market takes** | "BTC risk score is 0.359 today. Why I'm watching this number, not the price." | 3-5/week |
| **Builder lessons** | "I almost shipped a $500/month bug. Here's what I caught." | 1-2/week |
| **Education** | "What 'multi-factor risk' actually means in plain English. A thread:" | 1/week |
| **Personal narrative** | "Why I'm building a markets app in 2026, not 2017." | 1-2/week |

## Voice rules (the hard part)

Tonal guidelines specific to ArkLine — your audience is "serious individual investors," not crypto bros.

- **Be specific, not generic.** Not "the market is uncertain" → "BTC's 30D realized vol is at 32%, lowest since June." Specificity is credibility.
- **Show the work.** "Here's my actual chart" beats "I think it's heading down."
- **Skip the hype.** No "to the moon," no 🚀, no 100x language. Your audience wants signal, not vibes.
- **Quiet confidence.** "Here's what I see" beats "Here's THE truth."
- **No emojis on stat-heavy posts.** They cheapen the data. Reserve emoji for personal/lighter posts only.
- **Punchy, but not Twitter-bro.** Compress without sacrificing nuance.

## Weekly rhythm

Don't try to post 7 days a week. Aim for **5 active days, 2 dark.** Quality over volume.

| Day | What | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Weekly thesis (where you think markets are heading + why) | 30 min thread |
| **Tue** | Build update (yesterday's shipped work) | 5-10 min single post |
| **Wed** | Market take (today's risk score / signal / observation) | 10 min single post |
| **Thu** | Education thread (explainer on something concrete) | 45-60 min thread |
| **Fri** | Personal narrative / Friday reflection | 10-15 min single post |
| **Sat-Sun** | Light: 1 reply storm, no original posts | 15 min total |

That's ~3 hours of content production per week. Plus ~15 min/day of engagement (replies, quote tweets).

## Engagement playbook (the unsexy 50%)

Posting alone won't build an audience. You need to be *in* conversations.

**Daily, ~15 min:**

1. **Reply to 3-5 accounts** in your space who have more followers than you. Add value, not "great post!" Add a counterpoint, a data point, or an extension of their idea.
2. **Quote-tweet 1 thing** with your take — agree or disagree, but make it your perspective.
3. **DM 1 person** you'd like to know. Not a pitch. Just a "I noticed you're working on X, here's a thing I learned that's adjacent."

**Accounts to engage with regularly** (build a list of 30-50 to monitor):

Finance/crypto founders, macro analysts, finance Twitter veterans. Specifically look for:
- People building adjacent SaaS in finance
- Quants and risk analysts
- Macro commentators
- Crypto researchers (not influencers)

Don't engage with accounts way out of your tier (1M+ followers ignoring 100-follower accounts). Sweet spot is people with 5k-50k followers in your space. They're active, engage back, and signal-boost good takes.

## Workflow with me (Sunday check-in)

Every Sunday, ~30-60 min:

1. **You tell me:** what you shipped this week, what surprised you, what's on your mind about the market or the build
2. **I draft:** 5-7 post ideas covering the week's pillars
3. **You edit:** for voice, pick the 3-4 you actually want to post
4. **You schedule:** via Typefully or just post manually through the week

This way you produce a week's worth of content in one Sunday session. No daily "what do I post today" decision fatigue.

## Reactive workflow (when market events happen)

The Sunday plan is the **floor**, not the ceiling. When something big happens
in markets, you want to be early with a take — algos reward timeliness,
and being early to a thoughtful take builds reputation faster than
anything else you can do.

**When to drop the plan and react:**

| Event type | React? |
|---|---|
| Major price move (BTC/ETH ±5%+ in 24h) | Yes |
| FOMC/CPI/jobs report release | Yes, within 1-2 hours |
| Crypto ETF flow news (large inflow/outflow) | Yes |
| Major regulatory news (SEC, US Treasury) | Yes, after thinking briefly |
| Hack / exchange failure / depegging | Yes, with restraint (avoid speculation) |
| Notable hot take from finance Twitter | Maybe — only if you have a real counter |
| General "BTC up today" daily chatter | No — everyone has this take |
| Politics / culture wars | No, even when tempting |

**How to react fast — the 3-tweet framework:**

1. **The observation** (1 line, concrete): "BTC just dumped 7% in 30 min on no obvious news catalyst."
2. **Your unique angle** (1-2 lines, your perspective): "My risk score moved 0.31 → 0.42 in real time. That's the second-largest intraday risk move I've seen this year."
3. **What you'd do / what you're watching** (1 line): "I'm not adding here. Waiting for risk to settle below 0.4 before I move."

That's it. 3 tweets, ~5 minutes to draft. Don't overthink it. Speed > polish for reactive content.

**Ping me when it happens:**

When a market event hits and you want a fast take:

- Drop me whatever you saw, however disorganized
- I'll come back in <5 min with a 3-tweet draft using the framework above
- You edit for voice and ship

The bar for "is this worth my time" should be: *do I have a take that adds something to the conversation?* If yes, ship it within an hour. If no, skip.

**Reactive content metrics to expect:**

Reactive posts during major events typically get 3-5x more engagement than scheduled content — but only if you're early. If you're posting 6+ hours after the event, the algo has already moved on.

## Metrics to watch (the only ones that matter early)

Don't obsess over followers in the first 3 months. The vanity metrics will mislead you.

| Metric | Why it matters | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Profile visits** | Are people clicking through to see who you are? | 100+/week by month 2 |
| **Link clicks** | Are people clicking through to arkline.io? | Track via UTM |
| **Bookmark count** | Bookmarks = "I want to come back to this." Best signal of high-quality content. | Look for posts with >10 bookmarks |
| **Reply rate** | Are people engaging in conversation, not just liking? | 5-10 substantive replies per good post |
| **Waitlist signups attributed to Twitter** | The only number that pays the bills. | Track via UTM tag `?utm_source=twitter` |

Don't optimize for likes. Optimize for bookmarks, replies, and link clicks. Those convert.

## Don't do these things

- Don't buy followers
- Don't engage with engagement pods (it's transparent, kills authenticity)
- Don't post about politics or culture wars
- Don't dunk on competitors
- Don't reply to every troll — choose battles
- Don't post when angry; sleep on it
- Don't promote the waitlist in every post (1 in 5, maximum)
- Don't apologize for tweet frequency — nobody owes you reading time

## When to revisit this system

Re-evaluate after **30 days** of consistent posting. Look at:
- Which posts got the most bookmarks (do more of that format)
- Which posts got the most waitlist signups (do more of that topic)
- Where your replies are landing best (lean into those accounts)
- What you're burning out on (cut it)

The system isn't sacred. It's a starting structure. Iterate after you have data.
