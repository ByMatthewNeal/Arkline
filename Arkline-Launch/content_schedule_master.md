description in Google Calendar so the daily reminder stays in sync.

## Wednesday — Value Demo Day (the conversion engine)

This is the highest-leverage day of the week. One filming session feeds
content across all 5 platforms. The goal: show a real ArkLine signal
that mattered this week and what you did with it.

```
8:30am   Twitter thread (personal) — Value demo written version:
         "Here's what ArkLine showed me this week + what I did with it"
9:15am   LinkedIn post — Same story, longer narrative framing
9:30am–10:30am  FILM IG Reel (screen recording + voiceover):
         - Master cut: 60-75 sec Instagram vertical
         - Show the actual app, walk through the signal that mattered,
           narrate the decision you made and the outcome
11:00am  @Arklineio retweets personal thread
6:00pm   Post master Reel to Instagram
6:15pm   Repurpose: cut a 45-60 sec horizontal version for Twitter video
6:30pm   Post Twitter video with concise caption (different from thread)
9:00pm   Engagement: 5 replies

(Optional, if energy allows: design a 5-slide LinkedIn document
carousel re-telling the same demo as static slides. Schedule for
Thursday morning's LinkedIn slot instead of the standard educational
carousel that week.)
```

**Why Wednesday and not another day:** Mid-week is peak audience attention.
Monday/Tuesday people are catching up on weekend backlog; Thursday/Friday
they're checking out. Wednesday is your peak conversion window, so the
highest-converting content type belongs here.

**Build updates are not eliminated** — they still work as occasional
Twitter posts when you've shipped something genuinely interesting (a
major feature, a hard-won bug story, an architectural decision worth
sharing). Drop them into the reactive slot or a Saturday Stories drop
as needed. But they no longer earn a dedicated weekly slot — value
demos do, because they convert ICP traffic and build updates don't.

## Voice and format rules

See `twitter_system.md` for the full voice playbook. Quick reminders:

- **Be specific, not generic.** Concrete numbers beat vibes.
- **Show the work.** Charts > "I think."
- **No hype language.** No 🚀, no "to the moon," no 100x.
- **Quiet confidence.** "Here's what I see" > "Here's THE truth."
- **No emojis on stat-heavy posts.** Save emojis for personal/lighter content.
- **Don't pitch the waitlist in every post.** Max 1 in 5.

## The Repurposing Playbook (make once, distribute everywhere)

The single highest-leverage move in this whole system. One filming
session — especially the Wednesday value demo — feeds content across
all 5 platforms. Tweak the framing per platform, but the core asset
stays the same.

**Master asset:** A 60-75 sec Reel filmed on Wednesday (screen recording
of the app + voiceover walking through a real ArkLine signal that
mattered this week).

**Derivative cuts from that single asset:**

| Platform | Format | What changes |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram (personal) | Native Reel, 60-75 sec, vertical | Master cut — full version with music + captions |
| Twitter (personal) | Video post, 45-60 sec | Tighten hook to first 3 sec. Burn captions in. Square or horizontal crop. |
| LinkedIn (company page) | 5-slide PDF document carousel | Recreate as static slides: hook, the signal, the decision, the outcome, the takeaway |
| Instagram Stories | 3 single-message snippets | Cut key 5-10 sec moments. End with "Full demo on my feed" sticker |
| Twitter (personal) | Written thread | Tell the same story in text: "Here's what I saw / here's what I did / here's what happened" |

**Caption rules per platform:**

- **Instagram:** visual-first, music in mix, energy. Caption is short
  and emotional. Hashtags at bottom.
- **Twitter:** punchy, insight-dense. Hook within first 5 words. No
  hashtags. Drop the link in the second tweet, not the first.
- **LinkedIn:** longer narrative, "lesson" framing, no hashtag soup
  (3-5 max). Front-load the value in first 2 lines (the only part
  shown in feed).
- **Stories:** ephemeral energy. Casual. Use stickers/polls when fitting.

**What NOT to do:**

- Don't post the identical caption across platforms. Audiences notice
  and it reads lazy.
- Don't post the same length/format everywhere. A 75-sec vertical Reel
  dropped as-is on Twitter feels off — it needs the crop and the hook
  retime.
- Don't repurpose week-old content. Demos lose freshness fast in
  markets — a "what BTC did last week" Reel is dead by Friday.

**Time math:**
- Without repurposing: 5 platforms × 1 piece of original content each = 5x production time
- With repurposing: 1 filming session × 5 distribution surfaces = 1x production + ~30 min editing per derivative

Net savings: roughly 3-4 hours/week of production time, with the bonus
that every piece reinforces the same story (which is how brand memory
forms — repetition across surfaces, not single-shot virality).

## When to break the schedule

When a market event hits (BTC ±5%, FOMC, CPI, major regulatory news, ETF
flows, hack/depeg), drop the plan and ship a reactive 3-tweet take
within 1-2 hours. See `twitter_system.md` → "Reactive workflow" for the
3-tweet framework.

The Sunday plan is the floor, not the ceiling.
