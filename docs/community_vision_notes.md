# Arkline Community Vision — Early Notes

> **Status:** Exploratory thinking, not in v1 launch scope.
> **Date captured:** 2026-05-08
> **Trigger:** Matt's experience in Luke Davis's Slack community — life-enhancing access to smart people, exposure to how others think and invest.

---

## The strategic frame

Data alone is replicable. **Data + a small, high-trust community of investors anchored by a credible curator is not.**

Community could be Arkline's long-term moat — and could justify higher pricing. But if executed wrong, a generic crypto Discord directly contradicts the anti-influencer, serious, data-driven brand positioning.

The goal: replicate the *value* Matt got from Luke Davis's Slack (access to high-caliber people, learning how they invest, exposure to new data points) without the friction that plagues Telegram/Discord communities.

---

## What made Luke Davis's Slack work

Decoded ingredients:

1. **Anchor figure.** Luke is the gravity. People joined for him; they stayed for each other.
2. **Curated/gated entry.** Selection bias does most moderation work for free.
3. **Right-sized.** Big enough for interesting strangers, small enough for recognition over time.
4. **Mixed cadence.** Async by default, occasional real-time moments (calls, AMAs).
5. **Topic-anchored sharing**, not free-form gm/wen-moon noise.

---

## Translation to Arkline

You already have most ingredients quietly:

- **You're the anchor.** Your voice is on the website, briefings, risk-score framework.
- **150 founders is naturally curated and right-sized.**
- **The data gives natural sharing prompts.** "Here's what I think about today's risk score" beats "what's everyone watching."

---

## Concrete primitives to consider

### Build these
- **"How I invest" member profiles.** Manifestos with style, time horizon, data points watched, lessons learned. Evergreen content delivering "see how smart people think" value without real-time chat.
- **Member directory + DMs.** Where most "I met someone I wouldn't have" magic happens. Simplest piece to build.
- **Topic threads anchored to data** (briefings, risk scores, macro). Async-first, Slack-shaped but slower.
- **Monthly Matt-hosted live call.** Recreates anchor-figure energy. Members can show-and-tell. Recorded.
- **Curated weekly "Member Notes" digest.** You surface 5–10 standout contributions. Doubles as content marketing for next cohort.

### Don't build these
- Real-time public chat (becomes a moderation job, degrades quality).
- Free-form channels (channel sprawl = death).
- Public status badges/roles (invites the influencer culture you're against).
- Link-sharing without context (solves 80% of shill problem).

## Pragmatic build path

| Phase | When | What |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | v1 launch | NO community. Ship the app. Watch what founders ask for. |
| **1** | ~30 days post-launch with 30+ engaged members | Spin up private Slack or Circle (~$89/mo). Add member profiles + DMs + 1–2 topic threads. Test *content* of community before building *product* of community. |
| **2** | After signal in Phase 1 | Weekly digest. Monthly live call. |
| **3** | Only if Phases 1–2 prove demand | Build native in-app community. |

**Key principle:** Don't build community software in v1. Don't even build it in v2. Use Circle or private Slack until member behavior tells you what to build natively.

---

## Critical decision before committing

**A community is a multi-year personal commitment.** Without you showing up consistently as host/curator, even great members drift. Decide before committing whether you want this role for years.

If your bandwidth shifts later, the community design needs to shift with it (more peer-led, less host-led).

---

## Liability flag

If members give each other investment advice inside a product you host, exposure is greater than a standalone forum because Arkline itself provides market intelligence.

- Community guidelines should constrain to **discussion of data**, not specific recommendations.
- Raise this with the attorney during privacy/terms review.

---

## Open questions (revisit post-launch)

- Tiered access — is community part of the founding $400/yr, or a separate add-on?
- Does community access change the cap (currently 150 founders)?
- What does the new-member onboarding to the community look like? First-week experience matters most.
- Voice/tone of the community space — should match Arkline's serious data-driven voice, not crypto-Discord casual.
