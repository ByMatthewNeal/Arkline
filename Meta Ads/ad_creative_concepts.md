# Arkline — Meta Ad Creative Concepts (v1)

> **Purpose:** 10 ad concepts to test in Phase 1 (waitlist building) and Phase 2 (trial conversion). Concepts are ordered by production effort (lowest first). Pick 3–5 to launch with; iterate based on which hooks land.
>
> **Voice rules across all creatives:**
> - No emojis. No exclamation points. No "Don't miss out!" fluff.
> - Reading level: someone who reads The Information or Stratechery, not BroBros Live.
> - Always position against the alternative (YouTube algorithms, Twitter noise, gut-feel investing).
> - Numbers and specifics are good. Vague hype is bad.

---

## Strategic notes before reading

**Aspect ratios you'll need:**
- **1:1 (1080×1080)** — Feed (most important — Meta serves the majority of placements here)
- **9:16 (1080×1920)** — Stories + Reels (high impression volume, especially on mobile)
- **4:5 (1080×1350)** — Feed alternative (more vertical real estate, often outperforms 1:1)

Produce every static + carousel in **all three** aspect ratios. Meta's auto-placements will pick the right one per placement.

**Phase fit indicator:**
- 🟦 = Cold acquisition (broad audience, top-of-funnel)
- 🟨 = Retargeting (website visitors, custom audiences)
- 🟩 = Launch + post-launch trial conversion

## 1. "Today's BTC Risk" — data-driven static 🟦🟨

**Production effort:** Low (you can make this in Figma/Canva in 30 min)
**Format:** Static, 1:1 + 9:16 + 4:5

**Visual:**
Dark mode (Arkline brand). Big "0.42" number center-frame (or whatever today's actual reading is — refresh weekly). Small label below: "BTC Risk Score." A faint ghosted line chart in the background showing historical readings. Arkline wordmark bottom-left, small.

**Hook (the headline above the image):**
`Today's BTC risk: 0.42. Historically favorable accumulation.`

**Body copy:**
```
Most retail investors are guessing.

Arkline's 8-factor risk model gives you the same kind of read institutions use — on-chain behavior, technical regime, sentiment, macro context — distilled into a single 0–1 score.

iOS launch this spring. Founding pricing locked for the first 150.
```

**CTA:** `Get early access`

**Why it works:** Specific numbers grab attention in a feed of vague promises. The label "historically favorable accumulation" sounds analyst-y, signals seriousness. Refreshable creative — when the score changes meaningfully, you can ship a new version.

## 2. "Stop Investing on Influencer Takes" — anti-influencer static 🟦

**Production effort:** Very low (text-only design, 15 min)
**Format:** Static, all 3 ratios

**Visual:**
Pure dark background. Large white serif typography for the hook (mirrors your hero font on arkline.io). Subtle Arkline logo bottom-center. No imagery, no people. Looks like a Penguin Random House book cover.

**Hook:**
`Stop investing on influencer takes.`

**Body copy:**
```
Built by an investor tired of crypto Twitter noise.

Multi-factor risk scoring. Macro regime detection. AI-generated briefings. For retail investors who want signal, not screaming.

Arkline. Invest with conviction.
```

**CTA:** `Reserve early access`

**Why it works:** Identity-based positioning. You're not selling a feature, you're letting the viewer self-select ("I'm not one of those people"). Punchy hook works regardless of crypto market conditions. Cheapest to produce.

**Copy variants to A/B test:**
- A: "Stop investing on influencer takes."
- B: "The market doesn't reward the loudest. It rewards the informed."
- C: "Crypto Twitter is not your strategy."

## 8. "Noise to Data" — 15s video 🟦

**Production effort:** Medium (basic editing in CapCut or DaVinci Resolve)
**Format:** 9:16 (Stories/Reels primary) + 1:1

**Storyboard:**
- **0–4s:** Rapid cuts (every ~0.5s) of generic YouTube thumbnails, Twitter screenshots, crypto influencer faces. Audio: chaotic mash-up, building intensity. (Use stock or recreate vibes — don't show identifiable creators.)
- **5s:** Hard cut to silence. Black frame for half a second.
- **6–10s:** Arkline dashboard appears in dark mode, calm, slow zoom on the risk score number.
- **11–13s:** Text overlay (no voiceover): "Arkline. Invest with conviction."
- **14–15s:** CTA card: "arkline.io"

**Audio:** No voiceover needed. Music: starts chaotic, ends with a single low note holding. Lots of free options on Epidemic Sound or Artlist.

**Why it works:** Visceral contrast does the work in 5 seconds. The hard cut to silence is the hook. Works without sound (most feed video is muted).

## 9. "Founder's Note" — 30s video 🟦🟨🟩

**Production effort:** Low-medium (just iPhone + good light + clean background)
**Format:** 9:16 + 1:1 (square crop)

**Setting:**
Plain neutral background (white wall, bookshelf, or window light). Camera at eye level. You're wearing something simple (button-down or solid t-shirt — no Arkline merch, that reads "marketing"). Natural light from window if possible, no ring light.

**Script (memorize, don't read):**
```
For two years, I went looking for a tool that gave retail investors
the same kind of intelligence institutions have. Risk models, macro
context, AI briefings.

I couldn't find one. So I built it.

It's called Arkline. It launches on iOS this spring, capped at 150
founding members. If you're tired of investing on influencer takes,
get on the early access list at arkline.io.

Talk soon.
```

**Production notes:**
- One take is fine. Don't aim for polish — aim for authentic.
- Stop the camera between takes. Don't edit a "best of."
- Lower-third caption with `arkline.io` for the last 10 seconds.
- Caption the whole video (90%+ of feed video is watched muted).

**Why it works:** Founder-led video is the single highest-converting ad format for B2B/prosumer SaaS in 2024–2026 based on every ad agency case study I've seen. Authenticity beats polish. It's also the cheapest video format to iterate — film 5 versions in one afternoon, A/B test which one converts.

**Variations to film in the same session (5–10 min):**
- A: "Two years" version above
- B: "Anti-influencer" version — opens with "I'm not going to pitch you a Discord pump or a YouTube channel..."
- C: "Risk score" version — opens with "Today, BTC risk is at 0.42. Here's why that matters..."

## 10. "Morning Briefing Demo" — 20s screen recording 🟦🟨🟩

**Production effort:** Medium (screen recording + voiceover + cuts)
**Format:** 9:16 (vertical product walkthrough)

**Storyboard:**
- **0–3s:** Phone screen with notification: "Arkline: Your morning briefing is ready."
- **4–10s:** Open app, scroll through morning briefing — risk score, macro snapshot, top sentiment shifts.
- **11–16s:** Pause on a specific insight (e.g., "Risk-Off Disinflation regime detected"). Voiceover: "Arkline reads the macro backdrop so you don't have to."
- **17–20s:** End card: "Your morning briefing. arkline.io"

**Voiceover style:** Calm, low-key, almost monotone — like a Bloomberg news anchor, not a YouTube hype-bro. (You can do this; doesn't need to be professional.)

**Why it works:** Pure product demonstration. Cuts through skepticism by showing the actual thing. Highest converting for warm audiences who've already seen brand ads but haven't signed up.

## Recommended Launch Set (3–5 to start)

If you can only produce a handful before launching ads, prioritize in this order:

1. **#2 ("Stop investing on influencer takes")** — lowest production cost, strongest brand-positioning statement, works in any market
2. **#9 ("Founder's note")** — highest converting format, easy to film, builds trust
3. **#5 ("150 founding spots")** — scarcity engine, drives sense of urgency, refreshable as cap fills
4. **#1 ("Today's BTC risk")** — refreshable weekly with new score, gives algorithm new creative to test
5. **#6 ("Six pillars" carousel)** — comprehensive value-prop, retargeting workhorse

Concepts 3, 4, 7, 8, 10 are Phase 2 — produce after you have launch CPL data and know which messaging angles convert best.

## Production checklist per concept

For each concept you ship:

- [ ] Square (1:1, 1080×1080)
- [ ] Vertical (9:16, 1080×1920) — Stories/Reels
- [ ] Feed vertical (4:5, 1080×1350) — outperforms 1:1 on Feed often
- [ ] At least one **copy variant** for A/B testing the hook
- [ ] Filename convention: `arkline_concept-name_aspect_v1.{png|mp4}` (e.g., `arkline_youtube-takes_1x1_v1.png`)
- [ ] Saved to a shared folder so it's accessible when uploading to Meta Ads Manager
